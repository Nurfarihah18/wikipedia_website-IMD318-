History

Universiti Tun Hussein Onn Malaysia (UTHM) was established on September 16, 1993, under the name Pusat Latihan Staf Politeknik (PLSP). Initially, PLSP’s core mission was to train and produce polytechnic academic staff skilled in various engineering fields. The training institute was jointly managed by Universiti Teknologi Malaysia and the Ministry of Education Malaysia.

In recognition of the institution's success, three years later, it was upgraded to Institut Teknologi Tun Hussein Onn (ITTHO) on April 12, 1996. The official announcement was made by Dato' Sri Mohd Najib bin Tun Abdul Razak, who was the Minister of Education at the time. This upgrade acknowledged the institution’s excellence in producing human resources for technical education.

On September 27, 2000, the institution achieved another significant milestone when it was granted university-college status by the Malaysian government. The institution was renamed Kolej Universiti Teknologi Tun Hussein Onn (KUiTTHO), as a recognition of its contributions to science and technology-based development and its role in achieving national aspirations. The announcement was made by Tan Sri Dato' Seri Musa bin Mohamed, the then Minister of Education.

On September 20, 2006, the Malaysian government decided to grant KUiTTHO full university status. As a result, the name was changed to Universiti Tun Hussein Onn Malaysia (UTHM). This change was officially declared by Dato' Seri Hishamuddin Tun Hussein Onn on March 2, 2007, following the announcement by Datuk Mustapha Mohamed, the Minister of Higher Education on February 1, 2007.
